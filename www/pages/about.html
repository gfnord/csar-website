Who We Are

About Castlegar SAR

A team of dedicated volunteers protecting the West Kootenay region of British Columbia since the community established local search and rescue capability.

Our Mission

Serving Our Community

Castlegar Search and Rescue (CSAR) is a registered non-profit volunteer society operating as one of 78 Ground Search and Rescue (GSAR) groups in British Columbia. We are proud to co-serve the West Kootenay region alongside neighbouring SAR teams.

Our volunteers are unpaid professionals who donate hundreds of hours each year — to training, equipment maintenance, and responding to search and rescue incidents. We are available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, and can be tasked by the BC RCMP, BC Emergency Health Services, Fire Rescue Service, BC Coroners Service, and local municipalities.

All SAR services in BC are provided completely free of charge. There is never a cost to the person being rescued, regardless of the situation.

What We're Trained For

Our Capabilities

CSAR volunteers train extensively across a range of disciplines to handle the diverse and challenging terrain of the West Kootenay region.

🔍

Ground Search

Systematic ground searches using proven techniques, GPS navigation, and search management principles to locate missing persons in wilderness, rural, and urban environments.

🧗

Mountain & Rope Rescue

Technical rope rescue on steep and cliffy terrain, including lowering systems, high lines, and patient packaging for vertical environments in the Kootenay mountains.

🌊

Swiftwater Rescue

Swift water rescue operations on the Columbia, Kootenay, and other regional rivers and waterways — including bank operations, throw bags, and swift water swimming.

🚁

Helicopter Operations

Advanced helicopter operations including hover exit — allowing volunteers to enter and exit a hovering aircraft — essential for remote and difficult access rescues.

🩺

Wilderness First Aid

All members train in first aid and wilderness medical care, enabling them to assess and stabilize patients in the field until hospital-level care can be reached.

🦶

Tracking

Human tracking techniques to follow sign left by subjects in wilderness terrain — a critical skill for finding lost or disoriented individuals in the backcountry.

The West Kootenay SAR Network

Regional Partners

CSAR is part of a collaborative network of SAR groups across the West Kootenay and southeast BC. Teams regularly train together and provide mutual aid on larger incidents.

In 2023, Castlegar SAR hosted a major regional SAREX (Search and Rescue Exercise) in the Slocan Valley — drawing over 65 volunteers from 12 teams across southeast BC, including participation from the RCMP, Conservation Officers, and CASARA (Civil Air Search and Rescue Association).
