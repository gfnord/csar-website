Public Safety

Stay Safe Outdoors

The West Kootenay is stunning — but the backcountry demands respect. A little preparation can make the difference between a great adventure and an emergency call.

BC AdventureSmart

Know Before You Go — The Three Ts

BC AdventureSmart's Three Ts framework is your guide to safe outdoor adventure. CSAR strongly encourages every person heading outdoors to follow these principles.

T1

Trip Planning

Does anyone know where you are going and when you expect to return?

  • File a trip plan with a trusted person before you leave
  • Research your route — maps, elevation, hazards, and conditions
  • Check the weather forecast before and during your trip
  • Know how to call for help if your primary device fails
  • Set a definitive return time — and stick to it
  • Use the free AdventureSmart Trip Plan App
T2

Training

Do you have the knowledge and skills you need before heading out?

  • Take a first aid course before venturing into the backcountry
  • Learn basic navigation — map, compass, and GPS
  • Know how to recognize and respond to hypothermia
  • Understand the specific hazards of your activity (avalanche, swift water, etc.)
  • Know and stay within your own physical limits
  • Go with experienced people when trying new terrain or activities
T3

Taking the Essentials

Do you always carry the essential gear — and know how to use it?

  • Always carry the Ten Essentials, even on short trips
  • Bring more food and water than you think you'll need
  • Dress in layers appropriate for changing conditions
  • Carry a communication device — ideally a satellite communicator
  • Know how to use every item in your pack

Communication

Satellite Communicators (SEND Devices)

Satellite Emergency Notification Devices (SEND) — like SPOT trackers and Garmin inReach — are highly recommended for backcountry travel where cell coverage is unavailable.

These devices combine GPS with satellite messaging, allowing you to send your location and request help even from the most remote areas. Many also allow two-way messaging so you can communicate with family or respond to check-in requests.

If you activate the SOS on a SEND device, it triggers a response through the Joint Rescue Coordination Centre (JRCC) in Victoria, which notifies the appropriate local SAR group — including CSAR for incidents in our area.

What Happens When You Trigger SOS


  • Your position and SOS alert are sent via satellite to a monitoring agency
  • The alert is forwarded to the Joint Rescue Coordination Centre (JRCC) in Victoria
  • JRCC determines the response type and contacts EMBC
  • EMBC activates the nearest appropriate SAR group (e.g. CSAR)
  • SAR team responds and initiates search/rescue operations

Important: Only press SOS when you genuinely need rescue. Stay at your location if possible so rescuers can find you.

Seasonal Safety

Know Your Season

The West Kootenay presents different hazards year-round. Be aware of the risks specific to the season.

☃️

Winter

Avalanche hazard, hypothermia, frostbite, whiteout conditions, limited daylight. Check avalanche forecasts before backcountry travel. Carry an avalanche transceiver, probe, and shovel in avalanche terrain.

🌸

Spring

High and fast rivers from snowmelt. Unstable trails and washouts. Lingering snowpack on higher elevations. River crossings that look easy can be extremely dangerous in spring.

☀️

Summer

Dehydration, heat exhaustion, wildfire smoke, afternoon thunderstorms on ridges. Familiar locals are common SAR subjects — don't be overconfident on trails you know well.

🍂

Fall

Rapidly changing weather, early snowfall at elevation, slippery trails. Hunting season activity. Shorter days mean less time to self-rescue before dark.

If You Need Help — Call 911. Always Free.

Never hesitate to call for help because of embarrassment, cost, or fear of judgment. CSAR is here to help — no questions, no charge, no conditions. Your safety is what matters.
