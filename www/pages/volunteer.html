Get Involved

Volunteer With CSAR

Become one of the dedicated volunteers who keep the West Kootenay safe. No prior experience necessary — just commitment, enthusiasm, and a desire to help.

Why Volunteer?

Make a Real Difference

CSAR volunteers are the backbone of emergency response in the West Kootenay region. When someone is lost, injured, or in danger in the backcountry, it is our members who answer the call — day or night, in any weather.

Volunteering with CSAR is a deeply rewarding experience. You'll develop elite wilderness and rescue skills, form lasting friendships with like-minded people, and directly contribute to the safety of your community and the many visitors who come to enjoy the West Kootenay's incredible outdoors.

We are currently welcoming new recruits. With 35 active members and approximately 10 recruit positions available, now is a great time to join.

What We Look For


  • Genuine interest in outdoor recreation and adventure
  • Willingness to commit to regular training sessions
  • Ability to respond to call-outs (some flexibility required)
  • Physical fitness for demanding field work
  • Team player with good communication skills
  • BC resident in the Castlegar / West Kootenay area

SAR volunteers come from all walks of life. Prior SAR experience is not required.

The Process

How to Become a CSAR Volunteer

1

Reach Out

Contact CSAR through our Facebook page or email our Recruiter at recruiting@castlegarsar.ca to express your interest. We'll let you know about our current recruitment cycle and any upcoming information sessions.

2

Attend an Orientation

New recruits are invited to attend an orientation session to learn about CSAR, meet the team, and understand what's involved in volunteering with a SAR group.

3

Begin Your Training

Accepted recruits enter our structured training program, covering the core skills required for operational membership. Training is ongoing and held regularly throughout the year.

4

Become Operational

Once you've completed your initial training requirements, you'll become an operational member — eligible to respond to call-outs alongside the full team.

Skills You'll Learn

  • Search theory and field search techniques
  • Wilderness first aid and patient care
  • Rope rescue systems
  • Navigation and GPS use
  • Swiftwater rescue fundamentals
  • Helicopter safety and operations
  • Tracking and trailing
  • Incident Command System (ICS)
  • Field communication
  • Survival skills
More Ways to Help

Support Roles

SAR teams need more than field members. If you have skills to offer in other areas, we'd love to hear from you.

💻

IT & Tech Support

Help maintain our communications systems, website, databases, and other technology that keeps the team connected and organized.

📋

Administration

Administrative support including record keeping, meeting minutes, grant writing, and organizational governance are critical to a functioning SAR group.

🔧

Equipment Maintenance

Helping to maintain, inspect, and repair our vehicles, rescue equipment, ropes, and medical supplies keeps the team operational and safe.

📣

Public Education

Assist with community outreach, safety presentations, social media, and public awareness campaigns to help prevent emergencies before they happen.
