Who We Are

Volunteers Protecting the West Kootenay

Castlegar Search and Rescue (CSAR) is one of 78 Ground Search and Rescue groups across British Columbia, operating under the BC Search and Rescue Association (BCSARA) and tasked by the BC RCMP.

Our members are unpaid professionals who donate hundreds of hours every year to train, maintain equipment, and respond to emergencies across the rugged terrain of the West Kootenay. We serve Castlegar and collaborate with neighbouring SAR groups including Nelson, Grand Forks, Rossland, Arrow Lakes, Kaslo, Creston Valley, and South Columbia.

What We Do

Search. Rescue. Educate.

CSAR's work spans three core areas — responding to emergencies, training our volunteers, and helping the public stay safe in the outdoors.

🔍

Search & Rescue

We respond to calls from the BC RCMP to locate and rescue missing or injured persons in the field — on mountains, rivers, forests, and trails throughout the West Kootenay.

Stay Safe
Important

Search & Rescue is Always Free

There is absolutely no charge for search and rescue services in British Columbia — regardless of the circumstances, the outcome, or whether you went into a restricted area.

Accidents happen. Everyone deserves help. If you're lost or injured in the outdoors, please don't hesitate to call for help because of cost or embarrassment. Our members train for exactly these moments.

"We do our training to help people in need, regardless of why they got into that trouble."


📞 Call 911 for Emergencies

When to Call 911

  • Someone is lost and cannot find their way back
  • Someone is injured and cannot self-rescue
  • A person is overdue and has not returned as planned
  • You have activated a personal beacon (SEND device)
  • Any other life-safety emergency in the outdoors

Call as soon as possible. Stay in one location if you are able. Conserve phone battery.
