Preparedness

Training & Readiness

CSAR volunteers undergo hundreds of hours of training to be ready for any rescue scenario in the West Kootenay's challenging terrain.

Our Commitment

Training is the Foundation of Rescue

Every CSAR volunteer commits to ongoing training throughout the year. Our training program covers a wide spectrum of disciplines, ensuring our team is prepared for the diverse emergencies that arise in the rugged terrain of the West Kootenay — from technical mountain rescues and river crossings to wilderness medical emergencies.

Training takes place through weekly team sessions, specialized courses, and large-scale regional exercises like the SAREX (Search and Rescue Exercise) that brings together teams from across southeast BC.

Training Commitment


  • Regular weekly training sessions
  • Annual regional SAREX exercises
  • Specialized courses and certifications
  • Scenario-based field exercises
  • Joint training with RCMP & Conservation Officers
  • Helicopter safety training
Disciplines

Training Areas

Our volunteers train across the following core disciplines to maintain operational readiness.

🔍

Search Management

Systematic search planning and execution using Incident Command System (ICS) principles, probability theory, grid searches, and terrain association.

🧗

Rope Rescue

Technical rope systems for raising and lowering patients on steep terrain, including high-angle and low-angle rescue, patient packaging, and rigging fundamentals.

🌊

Swiftwater Rescue

River rescue techniques for the Columbia, Kootenay, and other regional waterways. Includes hydraulic rescue, throw bag operations, and swift water swimming.

🩺

Wilderness First Aid

First aid and patient assessment for backcountry environments where definitive medical care may be hours away. Includes hypothermia management, splinting, and trauma care.

🚁

Helicopter Operations

Helicopter safety and operations, including loading/unloading procedures and advanced hover exit technique — entering and exiting a hovering aircraft for remote access.

🦶

Tracking

Human tracking and sign cutting to follow subjects through diverse terrain types. A specialized and highly valued skill for locating lost or disoriented individuals.

🧭

Navigation

Map and compass navigation, GPS use, and terrain association. All volunteers must be proficient navigators to operate safely and effectively in the field.

📋

Incident Command (ICS)

Incident Command System training for SAR managers and team leaders, enabling effective coordination of multi-team operations and integration with RCMP and EMBC.

🏕️

Survival & Field Skills

Shelter construction, fire starting, signalling, and general wilderness survival skills — ensuring volunteers can operate effectively and safely in adverse conditions.

Regional Training

SAREX — Search & Rescue Exercises

CSAR regularly participates in — and has hosted — large-scale regional Search and Rescue Exercises (SAREX) that bring together multiple teams from across southeast BC for realistic, scenario-based training.

In 2023, Castlegar SAR hosted a SAREX in the Slocan Valley that drew over 65 volunteers from 12 teams, including Castlegar, Rossland and District, Grand Forks, South Columbia, Nelson, Arrow Lake, Creston, Sparwood, Elkford, Revelstoke, Oliver/Osoyoos, and Penticton.

The event included an all-day realistic scenario, swiftwater training, tracking, non-profit governance sessions, and a helicopter hover exit demonstration — called one of the best SAREX events ever by participants.

2023 SAREX Highlights

  • 65+ volunteers from 12 regional teams
  • All-day realistic multi-casualty scenario
  • RCMP and Conservation Officer participation
  • CASARA aircraft support
  • Swiftwater rescue training
  • Tracking technique workshops
  • Helicopter hover exit demonstration
