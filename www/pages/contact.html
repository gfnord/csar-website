Important

Don't Hesitate — Call Early

One of the most common mistakes people make is waiting too long to call for help. Calling early gives SAR teams more time to locate you before dark, before weather deteriorates, or before your situation becomes critical.

You will not be judged for the situation that led to the emergency. CSAR volunteers are there to help — not to shame or blame. Accidents happen to experienced and inexperienced people alike.

If you're unsure whether you need SAR help, call 911 anyway. The operator will help you determine the appropriate response. It is always better to err on the side of caution.

Tips When Calling 911


  • State that you need Search and Rescue assistance
  • Give your best estimate of your location (GPS coordinates if available)
  • Describe the nature of the emergency
  • State how many people are in the group
  • Report any injuries
  • Give your phone number so they can call back
  • Stay on the line as long as possible
  • After the call, conserve your phone battery
  • Stay in place if it is safe to do so
